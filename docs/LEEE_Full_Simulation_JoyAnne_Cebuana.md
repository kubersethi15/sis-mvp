### PHASE 3: STORY 1 DEEP DIVE

**LEEE [E102]:**
Got it. So ikaw mismo yung tumatawag? Who else was on your team?

**Joy Anne [M05]:**
Opo, ako po. Well, may mga kasama po kaming interns, mga 3 kami. Tapos yung supervisor namin si Ma'am Vicky. Pero yung calls mismo, kanya-kanya po.

**LEEE [P111]:**
Walk me through what happens pag may matawagan ka na mahirap. Like, anong nangyari sa pinaka-memorable na call mo?

**Joy Anne [M06]:**
May isang time po, may tinatawagan akong company to verify yung employment ng isang candidate. Yung HR person na sumagot, parang ang init ng ulo. Sabi niya "Bakit kayo tumatawag? We don't give out that information." Medyo natigilan po ako kasi ang lakas ng boses niya. Parang galit na galit.

**LEEE [P112]:**
Tapos ano ginawa mo?

**Joy Anne [M07]:**
Nag-breathe muna po ako ng deep. Kasi alam ko pag nag-panic ako, lalo akong mauutal. Tapos sabi ko po sa kanya, very calm lang, "I understand po na sensitive yung information. We're conducting a background verification for [candidate name] who listed your company as a previous employer. Would it be possible to speak with someone who can help us confirm dates of employment only?" Ganun po.

**LEEE [P113]:**
Ang galing. Paano mo naisip na ganun ang sasabihin? May script ba kayo or ikaw lang yun?

**Joy Anne [M08]:**
May template po kami na binigay ni Ma'am Vicky, pero yung sa situation na yun, hindi po siya exactly applicable kasi yung template namin is for when people say they're busy, not for when they're angry. So nag-improvise po ako. Naisip ko po na baka kasi masyado siyang nag-worry about data privacy, kaya in-acknowledge ko muna yung concern niya before I asked again. Natuto ko po yun sa Ma'am Vicky — sabi niya, acknowledge muna bago mag-request.

**LEEE [P124]:**
And then what happened? Paano mo nalaman na gumana?

**Joy Anne [M09]:**
Na-calm down po siya. Sabi niya, "OK, wait." Tapos ni-transfer niya ako sa ibang tao, yung head ng HR, and that person was super nice and gave me everything I needed. So na-complete ko po yung case na yun same day. Sabi ni Ma'am Vicky, "Joy, that was one of the hardest companies in our list, and you closed it in one call." Proud po ako dun haha.

**LEEE [X131]:**
Wow. Looking back, what do you think actually made the difference sa call na yun?

**Joy Anne [M10]:**
Hmm. I think po... yung hindi ako nagpanic. Kasi normally po pag may sumisigaw sa'kin, natatakot po ako. Parang natutulala. But that time, naisip ko, "This is my job. This person is not angry at me — angry siya sa situation." So na-separate ko po yung feelings ko sa situation. Yun po yung malaking difference.

**LEEE [X134]:**
Would you handle it the same way again?

**Joy Anne [M11]:**
Opo, same po. In fact, after that, pag may mga ganun na difficult calls, ako po yung ini-assign ni Ma'am Vicky kasi alam niya kaya ko na. So parang naging specialty ko po siya haha.

**LEEE [X135]:**
How did the other interns react?

**Joy Anne [M12]:**
Actually po, yung isang intern namin, she was really scared of the difficult calls. So tinuruan ko po siya nung approach na ginagawa ko — yung acknowledge muna, then reframe yung request. Nagtuturo po ako sa kanya during our break times. Si Ma'am Vicky natuwa po kasi sabi niya, "You're doing my job for me."

**LEEE [bridge]:**
That's a really great story, Joy. Yung pag-handle mo nung call na yun — ang galing. Gusto kong marinig isa pa. This time, something different — maybe not from work. Has there been a time when your plans completely changed and you had to adjust on the spot? Kahit sa bahay or sa school or anywhere?

**LEEE [E101]:**

Pero yung teammate ko na supposed to be the presenter, hindi po siya nagpakita nung actual day. Walang text, walang anything.

**LEEE [P111]:**
So anong ginawa mo?

**Joy Anne [M15]:**
Nagtaka po kami lahat. Tapos sabi ng trainer, "Someone has to present or your group gets zero." So... nag-volunteer po ako.

**LEEE [P120]:**
If I'd been watching that moment — anong makikita ko?

**Joy Anne [M16]:**
Haha, makikita niyo po na nanginginig yung kamay ko. Kasi po, honestly, that's my biggest fear — yung magsalita sa harap ng mga tao. Lalo na po kasi... may cerebral palsy po kasi ako, so minsan yung speech ko, hindi po siya ganun ka-smooth. And I know people notice. So yun po, sobrang kinakabahan.

Pero naisip ko po, "Wag na lang. Kung hindi ako, sino?" Yung mga teammates ko, mas takot pa po sila sa'kin. So kinuha ko po yung laptop, hinugas ko yung mga notes, and I presented.

**LEEE [P118]:**
Was there a part na hindi gumana or na-struggle ka?

**Joy Anne [M17]:**
Opo, sa gitna po ng presentation, na-stuck po ako. Nakalimutan ko po yung isang data point. Tapos nagtitigan po kami nung audience. Parang ang tagal, pero siguro mga 5 seconds lang yun. Tapos naisip ko, sabi ko sa kanila, "Honestly, nakalimutan ko yung exact number, pero ang point is this—" tapos ni-rephrase ko po yung idea without the number. And the trainer actually nodded. Sabi niya after, "That was smooth recovery."

**LEEE [X132]:**
What did you learn about yourself from that experience?

**Joy Anne [M18]:**
Na kaya ko pala. Like, all this time po kasi, iniiwasan ko yung mga ganyang situation kasi akala ko, hindi ko kayang i-handle yung anxiety plus yung speech ko. Pero nung nagawa ko na, parang... "Ah, it's possible pala." Hindi perfect, but possible. And yun po yung sabi ni Ma'am Vicky nung tinanong ko siya later — "Joy, you don't have to be perfect. You just have to be real." Tapos yun po, tanda ko pa rin hanggang ngayon.

**LEEE [X138]:**
What would have happened if you hadn't volunteered?

**Joy Anne [M19]:**
Zero po sana yung group namin. Three people's grades — sa isang tao na hindi nagpakita. So hindi ko po talaga pwedeng hindi gawin. And honestly, pagkatapos nun, yung dalawa kong teammates, sila pa yung nag-thank you sa'kin. Sabi nila, "Ate Joy, kung hindi ka nag-volunteer, wala kami." Nakaka-touch po yun.
